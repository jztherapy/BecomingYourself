Men's Counseling in New York

The wound is not the end of the story.

My approach draws on the men's work tradition — the idea that a man's grief, anger, and longing aren't weaknesses to manage but material to work through. In our sessions, that means going toward what you've been taught to avoid, not around it.

What Is Men's Work?

Descending before you can rise

A man does not become whole by climbing over his grief. He becomes whole by going down into it, and coming back up changed.
Schedule a Free Call

My approach is informed by the mythopoetic men's movement — thinkers and practitioners who noticed that modern men had grown cut off from initiation, from older men who'd done their own work, and from a grounded sense of what manhood actually asks of them.

Many of us were never truly welcomed into manhood by someone who could show us the way. We were left to guess, to perform, or to armor up. What gets labeled "toxic" in a man is often an unfinished initiation — grief, rage, or longing that never had a safe place to go.

In individual sessions, that means we go directly toward the things you've learned to avoid — not to dismantle you, but to help you become more fully yourself: less armored, more grounded, more real.

Common Themes

What men bring into the room

⟳

Life Transitions

Career pivots, divorce, fatherhood, retirement — thresholds that ask a man to become someone new, without a map for how.

◎

The Father Wound

The absence, distance, or harshness of a father — and the search for the recognition that was never given.

⌁

Anger & the Fire

Learning to hold a man's fire without being ruled by it, or burying it until it burns you from the inside.

⟜

Relationships

Showing up whole — with partners, children, friends — instead of hiding behind the armor that once kept you safe.

△

Grief & Loss

What has ended — a marriage, a career, an old identity — and the descent that has to happen before renewal.

◇

The Wound as Doorway

Early wounds and the stories they wrote about your worth — not to be fixed, but to be understood and worked through.
